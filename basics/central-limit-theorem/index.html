Central Limit Theorem

This is a simple demonstration of Central Limit Theorem. We have a set of input distributions that you can mix together to form an output distribution. The distributions are not finely sampled. But they are scaled to unit sum. The demonstration is not meant to be mathematically perfect. The aim is to show the changes in the final distribution.

Note that we are not 'adding' the distributions here. We are adding different, independent signals of same or different distributions. This results in the convolution of distributions
